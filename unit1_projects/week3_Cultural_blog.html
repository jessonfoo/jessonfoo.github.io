Cultural Blog: Your Thinking Style and Learning

I am an Abstract Sequential thinker and I have a Multimodal Aural Kinesthetic learning style.

Traits of an Abstract Sequential thinker

As a learner I like

  • to be heard out
  • analyzing situations before making a decision or acting
  • applying logic in solving or finding solutions to problems

I learn best when

  • When I have a experts to reference
  • placed in stimulating environments
  • able to work alone

Things I have trouble with

  • Being forced to work with those of differing views
  • Too little time to deal with a subject thoroughly
  • Repeating the same tasks over and over
  • Lots of specific rules and regulations
  • "sentimental" thinking
  • Expressing their emotions
  • Being diplomatic when convincing others
  • Not monopolizing a conversation


Application

Knowing that these things, I am excited to start interacting with people in real life, and having actual mentors at DBC. Having realized my weaknesses, I have been trying to correct the ones that might cause the most problems such as not monopolizing a conversation, and listening to others.


Experiences during phase 0 & hopes for the future

My biggest struggles during phase 0 related to my learning style has been having too little time to deal with subjects thoroughly, and working with a lot of specific rules and regulations. For example, when I was behind on week 2, I felt extremely stressed because I didn't want to just go through the motions of doing the challenges, but I felt like I had to which made me very anxious. What has helped me was the section on time management. The article I read about using unconventional methods of time boxing worked very well for me. I used it this week by giving myself an early due date for the assignments so that I would have time to go back later and review what I found difficult. The biggest change I want to make in terms of my mindset is how I approach Challenges, I want to embrace challenges when I feel like I am short on time. I also want to learn to see criticism as feedback that I can learn from.
